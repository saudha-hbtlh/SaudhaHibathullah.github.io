Video Games Market Trend Dashboard

In this project, delve into the history of the video game market from 1980 to 2017. Through data analysis and visualization with Microsoft Power BI, uncover the changing trends, patterns, and insights that have shaped the industry over the years.

Global sales page

The dashboard gives a lot of details about every game, like when it came out, who made it, what system it's for, and what type of game it is. People can look at all these things to understand how video games have changed over time.

Interactive Bar Chart- The dashboard features an interactive bar chart that allows users to drill down into specific topics and explore their connections. By clicking on a bar, users can delve deeper into the data and view sales figures for each topic, providing a clear understanding of the market dynamics.

Drill-Down Functionality- The bar chart includes a drill-through feature that enables users to navigate through different levels of detail. Whether examining sales figures by year, publisher, platform, or genre, users can easily explore the data and uncover meaningful insights.

Card Visual- The card will show how much money all the chosen options made worldwide in the dashboard. So, when people pick certain things like the year, who made it, what system it's for, or what type of game it is, the card will change to show the total money earned from all those choices. It updates automatically, so users can see the total sales without doing any math.

Sales Page

On the second page, you can see more about how much money video games make in different countries. This helps you look closely at how sales change in each place and understand the differences in how people buy video games around the world.

Drill Through Function- In the global sales page, when you click on a specific global sales detail, it will take you to the sales page. There, you'll see a breakdown of sales that occurred in each country. This allows you to explore how sales vary across different countries in more detail.

Stacked Bar Chart- Within the country-wise sales section, users encounter a stacked bar chart that visually represents sales data. This chart offers a clear and intuitive visualization of how sales are distributed across various countries. Users can explore sales data based on different criteria such as publisher, year, genre, and platform. This enables users to identify patterns and trends in video game sales across different dimensions.

Table- Another valuable feature of the sales page is a chart that displays the names of every game according to the chosen options. This chart provides users with detailed information about individual games within the selected parameters. Users can explore the titles of video games and gain insights into their performance based on various criteria such as publisher, year, genre, and platform.

Total Sales Summary Card- Surrounding the charts are cards that display the total sales figures based on the options chosen by the user. These cards serve as a quick reference point, summarizing the total revenue generated from video game sales according to the selected criteria.
